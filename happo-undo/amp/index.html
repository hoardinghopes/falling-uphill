Happo-undo

James Knight - 16 Feb 2018

A lovely class last night. Our Ki for Health and Ki Development exercises looked out for sensations of stretching under the movements, in rhythm with the count.

Then we analysed the turns in the 8-ways exercise (happo-undo) – the mind changes direction immediately and the body follows naturally, but without taking in the scenery. Thus each change is naturally faster and more powerful. It seems to invite greater clarity of mind too.

Second form (katatedori gyaku hanmi) yonkyo was our study for the rest of the lesson, both tenkan and irimi versions. Yonkyo done correctly is immediate and painless. Pain only occurs when the uke resists or bounces against the movement. If the nage seeks to create pain, his/her movement is weakened.

Finally, with the irimi version, we studied how stepping back gives the nage space and time to lead the uke. Once we understand the principles of yonkyo, we don't need so much time, so we experimented with immediate yonkyos with no stepping.

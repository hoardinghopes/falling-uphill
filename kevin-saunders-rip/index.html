Kevin Saunders RIP

1 min read

I heard, this afternoon, of another one passing. Not an aikidoka this time, but someone equally interested in the mindbody moving, and with great sensitivity too. I looked up his Facebook timeline to see whether there had been any forewarning of illness, but instead found this: carrying heavy luggage.

Two things struck me: (i) how we all carry heavy luggage of one sort or another, and (ii) how we all tend to freeze momentarily when attempting to improve our approach.

Kevin says that carrying luggage involves timing, distraction, load, and coordination, and lists the ways we are thwarted in these elements.

The relevance of what he's saying to Aikido is impossible to miss. We have to negotiate timing, load and distraction, whilst hoping to respond in a coordinated and artful manner. No small task!

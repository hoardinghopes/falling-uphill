13 April 2018 / Class Notes

Upside downside

1 min read
Also in: Aikido, 1st form, Kaitennage

It's been far too long since we studied kaitennage from the ground up, but last night we managed the whole journey.

Starting with creating the bow from one hand across the shoulders to the other, and testing to experience how that arc provides great strength with minimal effort, we moved on to forward rolling from kneeling - training the arc to bear our weight as it becomes a wheel, learning to move forwards rather than down.

Thence to rolling from a crouch, pushing off our rear foot rather than collapsing our front foot, again allowing the bow to roll us across the floor.

Finally: 1st form kaitennage - in the fog of numerous instructions and steps, the roll emerged as something natural and increasingly easy. Learning to generate the roll by ourselves is harder than being led into the roll by our partner.

It's been far too long since we studied kaitennage but, despite the rustiness, it was a delight, and I celebrated by being thrown continuously for the last 5 minutes. It seems fitness is something else I need to work on!

#theworldlooksbetterupsidedown

Why Ki Exercises?

James Knight - 20 May 2020

What is the purpose of ki exercises?

Ki exercises have a range of benefits, but their most physical effect is to take the slack out of our body. This is the process of improving the tonus of our tissues. According to Wikipedia, low muscle tone ("lax, flabby, floppy, mushy, dead weight‚Äù) increases flexibility but reduces strength; high muscle tone ("tight, light, strong") increases strength but reduces flexibility. This suggests that there is a useful middle ground of tone, with maximal balance between flexibility and strength.

So, when I say that ki exercises take the slack out of the body, I mean that they improve our tonus, the tone of our tissues (muscles, tendons, fascia). Once our tissues are toned, then they are in a position of maximum adaptability ‚Äì with a small increase in tone, they gain strength and stiffness; with a small decrease in tone, they gain flexibility.

How does that occur?

Language can quietly betray us, so I have to be very careful with how I phrase things here, and I invite you to be careful with how you interpret what you read.

Another way of saying ‚Äúremoving the slack‚Äù could be ‚Äútightening the body‚Äù. Most people tighten through contraction - by tensing their muscles. In ki exercises, we seek to do almost exactly the opposite: we aspire to relax our muscles as much as possible, whilst lengthening ourselves along various lines.

Think of a rope tied around two posts. If the rope is too loose, we have to remove the slack before it is useful to us. We can do that by pulling the rope tighter and tighter. This is what most of us do when we tighten the body ‚Äì we contract our muscles.

The other option is to leave the rope as it is, but move the posts further apart. This removes the slack without tightening the rope. It tensions the rope, without tensing the rope. We want to tension our tissues without tensing them.

In other words, we aim to remove the slack, to improve tonus, by expanding and extending our frame. As we lengthen our posture and our reach, we move the posts apart which tensions the muscles and tissues.

What does beginner look like?

Beginners start with learning the exercises as precisely as possible. Throwing a body shape is like putting up a tent. I can put up a tent in a short moment, using modern flexible tent poles and strategic placing of pegs. Then I can spend several days trying (never yet succeeding) to remove the rucks and slacks in the tent fabric by repositioning the poles and balancing the pull of guy ropes. Somewhere in that tent is an ideal position of all the poles, ropes, and pegs that removes all slack and leaves us a tent that is perfectly wind- and water-proof. I hope.

A beginner has to learn to put up their tent in a basic fashion - so that it stands by itself in a mild breeze - before they can work on removing the finer indicators of slack. Learn the exercises. Learn that gently bent elbows remove slack more than straight arms. Learn that thinking outwards along one‚Äôs arms removes slack more than just putting arms out.

A beginner should be aiming to move as precisely as possible by mirroring the teacher, i.e. they should be checking their movement against the visible guideline of the teacher‚Äôs movement and posture. (By contrast, someone with more experience should be using the felt experience of their posture and movement to improve it in order to remove slack further).

What‚Äôs after beginner?

Nothing. There‚Äôs only beginner ;-)

Having said that, with experience, a student should know the feeling they‚Äôre looking for and what conditions make that feeling more likely to arise. They should also know how to create those conditions.

Whilst we are always beginners in the sense that we should always be checking in with the model provided by the teacher (and the teacher should be providing that model, not just doing their own thing), increasingly we should become aware of another authority on the model:- our felt-sense, our experience.

So, a more advanced student does the same exercises and feels the slack leaving. Then they use their felt-sense to remove more slack. Then they have the potential to discover something new:- as tonus increases, so does sensitivity and awareness. Improving tone is the result of improving communication between brain, nervous system, and the tissues. As that communication improves more comes on-line.

Certain things can happen when our nervous system wakes up and fully fills our body. One of those things is that our body seems to become more transparent, as it were, more porous. The skin boundary dissipates as our nervous system reaches out further and more clearly into our proprioceptive space. Continuing further, that space expands, and our awareness fills what was previously (intellectually) thought to be gaps between people.
